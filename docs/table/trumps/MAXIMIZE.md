## 1. Core Identity

`MAXIMIZE` is a compelled hand-to-manifest trump.

It does not draw.
It does not reveal the board.
It does not wait for ordinary commit paths.

It lets the player force compatible cards
from `hand` directly into `manifest chain`
in any order and any quantity,
including repeated entry through the same column.

Short formula:

```text
play from hand directly into manifest
while topology allows it
stop when you choose
or spend yourself empty
```

## 3. Core Law

When `MAXIMIZE` becomes known:

1. You may reveal your `hand`.
2. You may play any number of cards from `hand` directly into `manifest chain`.
3. A play is legal only if the hand card is topologically compatible with the current manifest card in the position it enters.
4. A legal play replaces the current manifest card in that position by direct replacement law.
5. The replaced manifest card leaves by ordinary replacement law.
6. You may continue in any slot order.
7. You may use the same manifest position repeatedly, if each new insertion remains legal against the current card now occupying that position.
8. Stop when you choose to stop, or when no legal hand play remains.
9. After `MAXIMIZE` fully resolves, it follows ordinary trump ecology.

Short formula:

```text
hand -> manifest
legal if topology matches current slot
any order
same slot may be used again
stop by choice or exhaustion
```

---

## 4. Compatibility Law

For `MAXIMIZE`,
a hand card is topologically compatible with a manifest position
iff it shares at least one operator
with the current manifest card in that position.

This check is local.

It does not require:

- global chain matching
- left-to-right sequence preservation
- compatibility with every other manifest card

It requires only:

```text
shared operator with the card being replaced now
```

This is what allows one column
to be pressed repeatedly.

---

## 5. Direct Manifest Entry

`MAXIMIZE` uses the same kind of breach
as `Ôś│` on Circle 5:

```text
hand card enters manifest directly
```

This matters.

The card does not go through:

- target staging
- ordinary compiler waiting
- delayed install

It enters form immediately.

That is the whole violence of the trump.

---

## 6. Repeated Column Pressure

`MAXIMIZE` is not limited to one play per column.

If the new manifest card in a position
still permits another legal insertion from hand,
the player may press that same position again.

So the trump can:

- spread across many columns
- or consume one column repeatedly
- or empty the whole hand through a single legal channel

This is intentional.

Short formula:

```text
one column is enough
if it keeps accepting
```

---

## 7. Self-Exhaustion

`MAXIMIZE` does not protect the player
from emptying their own hand.

If the player spends the whole hand
through `MAXIMIZE`,
that risk belongs to the card.

The trump grants force,
not preservation.

This is load-bearing.

`MAXIMIZE` must remain able
to kill its own user indirectly
through overconsumption.

Short formula:

```text
the card may spend its bearer empty
```

---

## 8. Relation to GRANT

Without `GRANT`,
`MAXIMIZE` is bounded by the ordinary six manifest positions.

With live `GRANT` columns,
`MAXIMIZE` gains more entry surfaces.

This is strong,
but still bounded by:

- current live manifest space
- local compatibility
- available hand cards

So `GRANT` amplifies `MAXIMIZE`
without changing its law.

---

## 9. Why This Works

`MAXIMIZE` is strong because it breaks
the ordinary border between `hand` and `manifest`.

But it does not become trivial power,
because:

- compatibility is local and real
- the player can overconsume
- the trump gives no replacement hand material

It is not:

- free board rewrite
- simple burst draw
- safe tempo gain

It is compelled direct insertion.

---

## 10. Design Character

`MAXIMIZE` should feel:

- excessive
- compelled
- line-eating
- stronger than `RUSH`
- less controlled than `CANON`
- able to spend its own bearer empty

It should not feel:

- careful
- wise
- sustainable
- like ordinary efficient play

It is force without braking.
