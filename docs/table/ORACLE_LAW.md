# Oracle Law

Статус:

```text
canonical table law
adopted individual trump event
```

Legacy breadcrumb:

```text
II High Priestess
```

Core edge:

```text
▽ -> ☴
FLOW -> OBSERVE
```

## 1. Core identity

`ORACLE` is a controlled near-future reading trump.

It is not ordinary draw.
It is not ordinary observe.
It is not full tutor from deep deck.

It inspects the near incoming stream,
chooses one card,
and rewrites the immediate future order.

Short formula:

```text
look at top 6
take 1 into hand
return the rest on top in any order
```

## 2. Reading

`▽` here is not blind motion.

It means direct work on the incoming future stream.

`☴` here is not ordinary `observe`.

It does not create `known`.
It does not create `revealed`.
It does not trigger trump law
merely because a trump was seen.

So this trump reads as:

```text
select one path from the immediate future
without rewriting machine truth-state
```

This is not:

```text
search
```

And not:

```text
ordinary observe
```

It is:

```text
near-future shaping by selective sight
```

## 3. Core law

When `ORACLE` becomes known:

1. look at the top `6` cards of deck
2. choose `1` of those cards and put it into hand
3. put the remaining `5` cards back on top of deck in any order
4. after `ORACLE` fully resolves, `ORACLE` itself follows ordinary trump ecology

Short formula:

```text
top 6 -> inspect
choose 1 -> hand
rest -> top of deck in chosen order
ORACLE -> ordinary trump ecology
```

## 4. Look is not observe

This distinction is load-bearing.

`Look` here means:

- private inspection for selection
- no state upgrade
- no `hidden -> known`
- no `known -> revealed`
- no automatic trump trigger just because a trump was seen

So if one of the six viewed cards is a trump,
that trump does not automatically resolve
unless it is the chosen card
and later enters ordinary machine law by being taken into hand.

Short formula:

```text
look does not change machine truth-state
```

## 5. Why the remaining cards stay on top

The remaining `5` cards return to the top of deck
in chosen order.

This is intentional.

`ORACLE` should shape the immediate future,
not erase that future entirely.

It allows:

- near-future selection
- near-future ordering

It does not allow:

- bottoming unwanted future pressure
- deep future sterilization

Short formula:

```text
ordering yes
bottoming no
```

## 6. Restrictions

`ORACLE` does not:

- send viewed cards to the bottom of deck
- create `known` by looking
- auto-resolve trumps seen among the six
- access arbitrary deck depth beyond the top six
- count as ordinary `OBSERVE`

## 7. Relation to information law

`ORACLE` is intentionally adjacent to `OBSERVE`,
but not identical to it.

It should be read together with:

- [CARD_INFORMATION_STATE_LAW.md](./CARD_INFORMATION_STATE_LAW.md)
- [OBSERVE_VS_REVEAL_LAW.md](./OBSERVE_VS_REVEAL_LAW.md)

Division:

- `OBSERVE` = hidden -> known
- `ORACLE` look = private inspection without truth-state upgrade

## 8. Relation to trump ecology

`ORACLE` does not invent a separate ecology.

It uses:

- ordinary known-state trigger for `ORACLE` itself
- ordinary ordered resolution
- ordinary trump flow
- ordinary chain-close handling
- ordinary trump-zone law after its own resolution

Its specificity is not in ecology.

Its specificity is in shaping the near future
without rewriting truth-state.

## 9. Design character

`ORACLE` should feel:

- precise
- foresighted
- selective
- controlling without becoming absolute
- more like guided sight than brute tutor access
- less reckless than `FOOL`
- less violent than `RESET`
- less explicit than `EJECT`
