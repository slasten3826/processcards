# CHOOSE

Symbol:

```text
☳
```

Статус:

```text
canonical but delicate operator family
```

## 1. Core identity

`☳` = selection grammar.

It does not by itself promise payload.
It gives precision, narrowing, branching, or scope selection to another effect.

Short formula:

```text
☳ = choose / narrow / specify
```

## 2. What it is not

`☳` is not:

- guaranteed standalone payload
- generic damage/removal
- draw
- reveal

## 3. Weak law

Weak `☳` may intentionally be null.

This is allowed canonically.

Short formula:

```text
weak ☳ may do nothing by itself
```

That is not treated as a bug.

## 4. Strong law

Strong `☳` becomes meaningful through broader combined reading.

Strong baseline still applies:

```text
draw 2
```

`☳` matters most when the card also contains another operator.

## 5. Pair law directions

- `☳☴` — choose which hidden card to observe
- `☳☷` — choose what to dissolve
- `☳☵` — choose what hidden state to encode
- `☳△` — choose what to manifest
- `☳☶` — choose under one broken targeting restriction
- `☳☱` — choose what runtime-form scope applies

## 6. Physical execution law

Choose must point to visible legal options.

That means:

- options must be table-readable
- if a hidden choice is allowed, the class of legal hidden options must still be explicit
- no invisible engine-only branch list

## 7. Restrictions

- `☳` should not become free wildcard text
- pair law must define what is being chosen
- standalone null weak mode is allowed, but should not dominate the deck's value pattern

## 8. Open questions

- whether any self-pair `☳☳` payload should exist beyond duplicate law
- whether some weak `☳X` cards may inherit a tiny standalone branch effect
- exact UI wording later for null weak mode

## 9. Short formula

```text
☳ CHOOSE = precision operator, often meaningful only in combination
```
