# RUSH

## 0. Status

Working trump draft.

This is not final balance text.
This is not locked rules text.

Legacy breadcrumb:

```text
VII Chariot
```

Flavor text:

```text
What enters motion does not stop halfway.
```

Core edge:

```text
☷ -> ☳
DISSOLVE -> CHOOSE
```

---

## 1. Core Identity

`RUSH` is a momentum trump.

It does not optimize.
It does not filter.
It does not stop to inspect.

It injects hand material at speed.

Short formula:

```text
forward burst of hand-state
```

---

## 2. Reading

`☷ DISSOLVE` here is not ordinary discard.

It is the dissolution of friction:

```text
delay
hesitation
intermediate obstruction
```

`☳ CHOOSE` here is not delicate tactical selection.

The choice has already happened:

```text
direction = forward
```

So this trump reads as:

```text
resistance stops mattering
because direction has already been chosen
```

This is not:

```text
survey
sorting
route optimization
```

It is motion already underway.

---

## 3. Core Law

When `RUSH` becomes known:

1. Draw 6 cards from deck, one by one.
2. If a drawn card is a trump, reveal it and queue it by the ordinary trump draw law.
3. Drawn trumps do not interrupt `RUSH`.
4. After `RUSH` fully finishes, queued trumps resolve in normal order.

Short formula:

```text
draw 6
queued trumps wait
resolve them after RUSH
```

---

## 4. Why This Is Draw 6

The effect is intentionally:

```text
draw 6
```

Not:

```text
draw up to hand size
draw and filter
draw and discard
```

Reasons:

- direct
- physical
- high tempo
- no pause for analysis
- depth happens after the burst, not inside it

`RUSH` should feel like:

```text
mass enters hand faster than the player can domesticate it
```

---

## 5. Hand-State Role

There is currently no hand limit.

So `RUSH` is not refill.
It is pure addition.

That is acceptable here.

This trump is allowed to create:

- oversized hand states
- greedy follow-up turns
- bad overextension
- self-inflicted chaos

Especially on unstable floors,
this can become dangerous instead of purely good.

Short formula:

```text
RUSH gives material
it does not promise control
```

---

## 6. Relation to Trump Ecology

`RUSH` should not invent a special trump-draw exception.

It uses the ordinary global draw law:

- draw one by one
- drawn trumps are exposed
- exposed trumps are queued
- queued trumps wait until `RUSH` ends
- then they resolve by normal order

This matters.

`RUSH` should be a burst card,
not a private ecology rewrite.

---

## 7. Why This Works

Not every trump should be a topology machine.

Some trumps should be strong because they are blunt.

`RUSH` belongs to that class.

Its depth comes from:

- when it appears
- what hand-state it creates
- what queued trumps follow it
- how much instability the player can survive afterward

Not from internal procedural complexity.

---

## 8. Design Character

`RUSH` should feel:

- direct
- physical
- forceful
- fast
- reckless
- momentum-first
- stronger in unstable contexts

It should not feel:

- analytical
- careful
- curated
- selective
- logical
